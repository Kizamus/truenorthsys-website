TrueNorth Sourdough Guide

A mobile-first baking companion that combines a recipe calculator, step-by-step instructions, rest timers, and saved progress for a calmer sourdough bake.

Open the guide

What the guide does

The TrueNorth Sourdough Guide helps a baker choose a target loaf size and dough hydration, then calculates the ingredient amounts needed for that bake. It then walks through the full process, from waking the starter to cooling the finished loaf.

Sourdough can be confusing because time alone is not enough. The guide includes visual and tactile checkpoints such as starter rise, dough bubbles, bulk fermentation signs, shaping tension, crust color, and hollow sound after baking.

Calculator

Ingredient amounts update from the selected loaf weight and hydration percentage, including flour, water, starter, salt, and dough weight.

Guided stages

The bake is split into starter, mixing, folds, bulk fermentation, shaping, cold proofing, and baking.

Timers

Rest and bake timers help the user follow folding and oven stages without needing a separate timer app.

Saved progress

The guide saves progress locally in the browser so a user can reopen the app and continue their bake.

Who it is for

The guide is written for beginner and intermediate home bakers who want a practical process rather than a long article. It is especially useful for bakers who understand the basic idea of sourdough but want help with quantities, timing, and judging dough readiness.

Does the guide require an account?

No. The guide does not require an account. Bake progress is stored in the user's browser using local storage.

Is the app mobile friendly?

Yes. The guide is designed mobile and tablet first because baking usually happens away from a desktop computer.

Does the guide replace baking judgement?

No. It supports judgement by explaining what to look for at each stage. Sourdough still depends on starter strength, room temperature, flour, and handling.
