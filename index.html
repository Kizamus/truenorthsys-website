What TrueNorth Builds

Small tools for clear everyday decisions.

TrueNorth Systems is an independent software studio focused on useful web apps, calculators, guides, and workflow helpers. The goal is not to build noisy software with endless menus. The goal is to take a practical task, understand the decisions inside it, and turn those decisions into a calmer, easier experience.

Practical by default

Every project starts with a real workflow: what someone needs to know, what they need to calculate, and what they need to do next. Features are added when they remove friction, not just because they can be built.

Designed for repeated use

The apps are made for people who return to a task over time. That means clear labels, predictable controls, saved progress where it matters, and layouts that work well on phones and tablets.

Useful before clever

Why a sourdough guide?

Sourdough baking is full of small decisions: how much flour and water to use, when the starter is ready, how long to rest the dough, what a good bulk ferment looks like, and when to bake. Many recipes assume the baker already understands those signals.

The TrueNorth Sourdough Guide turns that process into a practical workflow. The calculator adapts ingredient amounts to the loaf size and hydration selected by the user, while the guide breaks the bake into clear stages: starter, mixing, folds, bulk fermentation, shaping, cold proofing, and baking. The app stores progress locally in the browser, so the user can leave the page and come back without losing their place.

This is the kind of tool TrueNorth Systems is built around: a focused app that takes a task with uncertainty and gives the user a dependable path through it.

How The Guide Helps

Built around the baking workflow.

Recipe calculation

The app calculates flour, water, starter, salt, and estimated dough weight from the selected loaf size and hydration percentage.

Step checkpoints

Each stage includes short instructions and visible signs to look for, so the baker can judge the dough instead of relying only on time.

Rest timers

Folding and baking stages include timers with an audible alarm and vibration support where the browser allows it.

Local progress

The guide stores bake progress in local browser storage for convenience. No account is required and the data stays on the user's device.
